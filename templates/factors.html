A Look at the Factors

Factors' Correlation with World Happiness


Select a view




Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is the annual value of all goods and services a country produces. Each country independently calculates this number, normally with assistance from a national government statistical agency.

The same survey results of the Gallup World Poll defines Social Support. The average of all survey answers determines the outcome of this factor for each country.

The estimate for Life Expectancy represents the average number of "healthy" years a child at birth is estimated to live. The World Health Organization (WHO) calculates this based on over 100 different health factors.

Freedom is based on the average response to the Gallup World Poll question: "Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?"

Generosity is based on the response to the Gallup World Poll question: "Have you donated money to a charity in the past month?"

Government Corruption is based on the average response to the following 2 questions: 1) "Is corruption widespread throughout the government?" and 2) "Is corruption widespread within businesses?"

Perceptions of Happiness

As the world advances, so do citizens' perceptions of happiness

The visualization shows the relationship between life expectancy and GDP per capita, and their combined effect on Happiness scores in an interactive time-adjustable interface. We can learn which countries have either suffered or prospered as a result of changes in GDP per capita and life expectancy, which is a variable that represents a combination of factors that contribute to quality of life, such as access to healthcare, crime rates, infant mortality, economic development, and improvement in environmental conditions.
